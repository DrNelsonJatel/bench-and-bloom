# Bench & Bloom — Pricing & Break-Even (2026 pilot)

Lavender Simple Syrup, 250 mL · Amazon.ca FBA · **chosen price $26.99 CAD**

## Assumptions (edit these as real numbers come in)
| Input | Value |
|---|---|
| Landed COGS / bottle | $4.65 |
| Your labour / bottle | $1.50 |
| Amazon referral (Grocery) | 15% of price |
| FBA fulfilment / bottle | $5.00 |
| Storage + inbound / bottle | $1.50 |

## COGS breakdown (per bottle)
| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Glass bottle (Uline S-23397) | $1.70 |
| Inbound freight (allocated) | $0.87 |
| Cap (28-400, to order) | $0.15 |
| Shrink band | $0.03 |
| Labels (front + back, BOPP) | $1.00 |
| Syrup (sugar + butterfly pea + lavender) | $0.90 |
| **Landed COGS** | **$4.65** |

## Profit by price point
| Price | Referral | FBA | Stor/in | COGS | Profit (no labour) | Margin | Profit (w/ labour) | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $22.99 | $3.45 | $5.00 | $1.50 | $4.65 | $8.39 | 36% | $6.89 | 30% |
| $24.99 | $3.75 | $5.00 | $1.50 | $4.65 | $10.09 | 40% | $8.59 | 34% |
| **$26.99** | $4.05 | $5.00 | $1.50 | $4.65 | **$11.79** | **44%** | $10.29 | 38% |
| $28.99 | $4.35 | $5.00 | $1.50 | $4.65 | $13.49 | 47% | $11.99 | 41% |

Formula: `profit = price − (15% × price) − $5.00 − $1.50 − $4.65`.
Break-even ≈ **$13.10** (no labour) / **$14.90** (with labour).

## Pilot 2026 — two cases (48 bottles) @ $26.99
- Revenue if all sell: **$1,295.52**
- Gross profit (no labour): ~$566 · (with labour): ~$494
- Less one-time costs (GS1 GTIN ~$40, label minimum, samples): −~$100–200
- A validation run, not a profit centre. Per-unit allocations are high at 48 units and drop with volume.

## Market
Okanagan Lavender & Herb Farm $20 / 250 mL · Sonoma ~$20–25 · premium Amazon syrups $18–26 · commodity (Monin/Torani 750 mL) $10–15.

## Before locking the price
1. Run Amazon's **Revenue Calculator** with the exact packed bottle (FBA fee moves the math most).
2. Confirm the **Grocery referral %** (some items get 8% on a portion).
3. Order the **28-400 caps** (not in the Uline order).
4. Re-plug the **real label quote** once you have it.
