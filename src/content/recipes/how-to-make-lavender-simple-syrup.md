ingredients:
  - 1 cup water
  - 1 cup cane sugar
  - 1 to 2 teaspoons dried culinary lavender buds
steps:
  - Combine the water and sugar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  - Bring just to a simmer, then take the pan off the heat.
  - Stir in the lavender buds, cover, and steep. Start tasting at 5 minutes.
  - The moment it reads floral and not sharp, strain out the buds through a fine sieve.
  - Cool completely, then bottle and refrigerate.

Lavender simple syrup is the base for every drink in this collection. It is three
ingredients and about twenty minutes, and the only hard part is the steep.

A note on the lavender: use real culinary lavender buds, never essential oil, which is
not safe to drink. We grow **Grosso**, a bold, aromatic variety, so we use the lighter
end of the range. A softer English lavender can take a little more. Either way, taste as
you go.

## The one trick

Over-steeping is what turns lavender soapy. Start tasting at five minutes and stop the
moment the syrup reads floral, before it turns sharp or medicinal. With a bold lavandin
like Grosso that can be as little as five to seven minutes.

Store it in a sealed jar in the fridge for about a month. Or skip the steeping entirely:
a bottle of [Bench & Bloom](/syrup) is this syrup, already dialled in.

Once you have a batch, pour it into a [Lavender Gin Fizz](/recipes/lavender-gin-fizz), a
[French 75](/recipes/lavender-french-75) or a [Lavender Lemonade](/recipes/lavender-lemonade).
