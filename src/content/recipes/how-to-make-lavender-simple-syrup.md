ingredients:
  - 1 cup water
  - 1 cup cane sugar
  - 1 to 2 teaspoons dried culinary lavender buds
  - 1 tablespoon dried butterfly pea flowers (for the natural blue)
steps:
  - Combine the water and sugar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  - Bring just to a simmer, then take the pan off the heat.
  - Stir in the lavender buds and butterfly pea flowers, cover, and steep. Start tasting at 5 minutes.
  - The moment it reads floral and not sharp, strain out the buds and flowers through a fine sieve.
  - Cool completely, then bottle and refrigerate. It will be a deep blue.

Lavender simple syrup is the base for every drink in this collection. It is four ingredients and
about twenty minutes, and the only hard part is the steep.

A note on the lavender: use real culinary lavender buds, never essential oil, which is not safe to
drink. We grow **Grosso**, a bold, aromatic variety, so we use the lighter end of the range. A softer
English lavender can take a little more. Either way, taste as you go.

The **butterfly pea flower** is what makes it blue. It adds almost no flavour, just a deep natural
colour and a trick: the blue is pH sensitive. Add a squeeze of lemon, a splash of tonic or a pour of
sparkling wine and it blooms from blue to pink. No dye, just chemistry.

## The one trick

Over-steeping is what turns lavender soapy. Start tasting at five minutes and stop the moment the
syrup reads floral, before it turns sharp or medicinal. With a bold lavandin like Grosso that can be
as little as five to seven minutes.

Store it in a sealed jar in the fridge for about a month. Or skip the steeping entirely: a bottle of
[Bench & Bloom](/syrup) is this syrup, already dialled in, with our own estate-grown Grosso.

Once you have a batch, pour it into a [Naramata Sunset](/recipes/naramata-sunset), a
[Lavender Gin Fizz](/recipes/lavender-gin-fizz) or a [Lavender Lemonade](/recipes/lavender-lemonade).
